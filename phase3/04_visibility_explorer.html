The visible and the hidden

A museum decides what to spotlight. In the catalogue, a handful of objects are flagged as "highlights" or placed on curated timelines. Everything else is simply held. This is what that ratio actually looks like.

Variables: visibility_group (from is_highlight × is_timeline_work) × department × time period. Each dot is one object.

The visible fraction, department by department

Each bar is one department. The coloured sliver on the left is the share flagged as highlight or timeline work. It never grows large — the spotlight is narrow everywhere.

Roughly 97 in every 100 objects are "regular" — never highlighted, never placed on a timeline. The collection the public meets through highlights and timelines is a tiny, curated surface over a vast, largely unseen body. Making that ratio literal — one dot per object — is knowledge a table of counts hides.

Source: The Metropolitan Museum of Art Open Access dataset, public-domain working subset (30,000 objects). Visibility derived from the is_highlight and is_timeline_work flags.
