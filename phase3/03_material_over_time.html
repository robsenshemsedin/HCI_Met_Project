What the collection is made of

Every object is some material — paper, metal, clay, stone, cloth. Laid out across time, the collection's material changes character completely from one era to the next.

Variables: medium_group (11 material classes) × time_period_group (8 eras). Each band is a material; its thickness is that material's share of the era.

Show:

Read left to right and the collection changes substance: the ancient eras are ceramic, metal and stone — durable things that survive burial. The modern eras are overwhelmingly paper — prints and drawings. The collection isn't a steady accumulation of "art"; it's a record of what each era made, what survived, and what a museum chose to keep.

Why share, not count?

The raw-count view (toggle above) is dominated by the huge 1800–1899 paper bucket, which flattens every other era. Switching to share-of-era makes the mix comparable, so the material character of antiquity is visible next to that of the 19th century.
