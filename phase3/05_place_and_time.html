Culture · Period

This is the honest version of "seeing a time and place": nothing is invented or reconstructed — every object is really in the collection, shown at its real image, linked to its real record. The gaps are meaningful too. Greek, Etruscan and Coptic works sit in a single era; Chinese and Japanese objects stretch across many. What a museum holds from a culture is never evenly spread across time.
