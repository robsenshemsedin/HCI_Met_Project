The unlocated collection

Most maps of a museum collection quietly drop the objects that have no place attached. This one does the opposite: it counts them.

Variables: has_geography_info (country / region / culture present?) × department × time period.

Where the gap lives — by department

Each bar is one department, scaled to its share of the subset. The terracotta portion is objects the catalogue cannot place on a map. The gap is not spread evenly — it concentrates.

The gap over time

Older material is far more likely to be unlocated: provenance thins out the further back you go.

A map drawn only from the located 60% would look authoritative and complete. It would also be a quiet fiction. Showing the missing 40% is the more honest representation — and it turns "missing data" into a finding about how the collection was catalogued.

Source: The Metropolitan Museum of Art Open Access dataset, public-domain working subset (30,000 objects, stratified by department). Geography counted present if any of country, region or culture is recorded. · Robsen Shemsedin Yusuf · CM0482, a.y. 2025–26.
